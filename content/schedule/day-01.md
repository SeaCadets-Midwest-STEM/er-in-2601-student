## Theme: Firmware Introduction, Programming Logic, Control Structures, Hardware I/O

Welcome to the world of embedded systems. On Day 1, you'll learn how software talks to hardware by writing your first firmware programs for the Arduino Uno Q at your team's Creation Station.

### Sessions

- **"The Ghost in the Machine"** - Motion Control Hook Demo
- **Station Orientation** - Meet your Creation Station and learn the tools at your fingertips
- **Code Sneak Peek** - Build the mental model: code is just instructions
- **Hello Matrix** - Wire an LED and write your first sketch using the onboard 8&times;13 RGB LED matrix
- **Control Structures** - Loops, delays, and patterns
- **Button Input** - If/else logic and hardware decisions
- **The Firmware Arcade** - Tiered challenge lab to test everything you've learned

### Key Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Firmware | Software that runs on microcontrollers to control hardware |
| Sketch | An Arduino program written in C/C++ |
| Digital I/O | Pins that read or write discrete HIGH or LOW signals |
| Arduino Uno Q | A dual-brain board (Qualcomm Dragonwing + STM32) running Debian Linux with an onboard 8&times;13 RGB LED matrix |
| Creation Station | Your team's dedicated workstation with an Arduino Uno Q, breadboard, components, and power supply |

### What to Bring

- Yourself and your team - all equipment is at your Creation Station
- Curiosity and a willingness to debug!
