## Theme: Sensors, ADC, Analog Input, PWM, and Breadboard Circuits

On Day 2, your boards will learn to sense the world around them. You'll explore how real-world signals become digital data through sensors, analog-to-digital conversion, and breadboard circuits.

### Sessions

- **"The Blind Robot"** - Sensor Challenge Hook Demo
- **Sensor Fundamentals** - How sensors work and why they matter in embedded systems
- **Analog-to-Digital Conversion (ADC)** - Turning continuous signals into numbers the microcontroller can read
- **LDR Light Sensor** - Reading ambient light levels with a photoresistor
- **Thermistor Temperature Sensor** - Measuring temperature changes
- **PWM and Brightness Control** - Pulse-width modulation for smooth LED dimming and servo control
- **Hysteresis and Thresholds** - Preventing flickering outputs with smart decision logic
- **Breadboard Circuit Builder** - Hands-on lab wiring multiple sensors into a complete monitoring circuit

### Key Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| ADC | Analog-to-Digital Converter: transforms continuous voltage into discrete numeric values |
| PWM | Pulse-Width Modulation: rapidly switching a pin on/off to simulate analog output (e.g., dimming LEDs) |
| LDR | Light-Dependent Resistor: a resistor whose resistance decreases with increasing light |
| Thermistor | A temperature-sensitive resistor used to measure heat changes |
| Hysteresis | A technique that prevents rapid toggling by requiring different thresholds for turning on vs. off |
| Breadboard | A solderless platform for prototyping circuits with interconnected internal wires |
